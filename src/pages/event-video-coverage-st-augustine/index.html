Music Festival Event Coverage

September 2025 · Sing Out Loud Festival, St. Augustine

First Sight Films has covered the Sing Out Loud Festival for three years running — 2023, 2024, and 2025. Sing Out Loud is produced by SJC Cultural Events (SJCCE), the St. Johns County nonprofit that also runs the St. Augustine Amphitheatre and the Ponte Vedra Concert Hall — so the same team that trusts us with the festival trusts us with their venues.

The festival comes in two parts, and we cover both. The Local Showcase runs all September across downtown St. Augustine — free shows at multiple venues. The Live Wildly Showcase is the paid two-day main event at Francis Field, headlined by major national acts.

We staff it with a 4–5 person crew — videographers, a drone operator, and photographers — and we help SJCCE plan the content capture so the turnaround can keep up with the event. We deliver a recap film for each day within 48 hours, cut multiple 15–20 second social clips from every day (drone shots of the stage during the headliner, the big moments), and edit photos same-day — pulling images right after an act steps off stage so the festival can post that night. In 2025 we delivered roughly 400 edited images.

The work doesn't stop when the festival does. SJCCE stores our footage and reuses it for the next year's paid ad campaigns and organic social. Our festival and concert photography ran with local news coverage in the Ponte Vedra Recorder and the Jacksonville Business Journal — and on the festival's own Instagram, our photos drove 13,000 likes and impressions in three days against a 25,000-follower account.

For scale: the 2025 festival drew over 34,000 people across the month, with 25,000 at the two-day Live Wildly Showcase, generated an estimated $9 million in local economic impact, and raised more than $130,000 for local land conservation. We're the team SJCCE trusts to document it.

Cultural & Heritage Event Coverage: Fort Mose Jazz & Blues

February 2025 · Fort Mose Historic State Park

First Sight Films covered the Fort Mose Jazz & Blues festival for the St. Johns Cultural Events team — a night concert at Fort Mose Historic State Park, the site of the first free Black settlement in what's now the United States. The setting carries weight, and the coverage had to hold both the music and the history.

We ran a multi-camera setup on the performances and worked the grounds for the crowd, the dusk light, and the details that tie the night to the place. Because the event runs after dark, low-light shooting was the technical challenge — exposing for stage lighting while keeping the venue readable.

We delivered a recap film plus social cuts the team could run across their channels to carry the event's reach past the one night and into the next year's promotion. Read more about what it takes to capture live music at Fort Mose Jazz & Blues.

Ribbon Cuttings & Grand Openings

March 2026 · St. Augustine & St. Johns County Visitor Information Center

When the Consulate General of Ireland in Miami needed video for the opening of their St. Augustine exhibit, they Googled "event videographer in St. Augustine" and reached out to three of us. Two didn't answer for three weeks. First Sight Films answered the same day — and got the job.

First Sight Films filmed the opening of the exhibit on the Irish diaspora in St. Augustine at the St. Augustine & St. Johns County Visitor Information Center in March 2026. The exhibit tells the story of the oldest known St. Patrick's Day parade in the Americas — which started right here in St. Augustine in 1600. The Consulate flew its speakers in from Ireland for the opening, including Irish Government Minister Niamh Smyth and faculty from University College Dublin and the Royal Irish Academy of Music.

We covered it with two cameras — the full remarks from the keynote speakers, the musical performances, and attendees taking in the new exhibit, plus b-roll throughout. We delivered two pieces: a 30-second social reel built to advertise the exhibit and drive attendance, and a full keynote video of the two main speakers — two camera angles, professional audio, fully captioned.

The Consulate ran our reel as a paid advertising asset for a month-long campaign promoting the exhibit.
