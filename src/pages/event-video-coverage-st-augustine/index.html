Music Festival Event Coverage

Project at a glance

  • Client: SJC Cultural Events (also runs the St. Augustine Amphitheatre + Ponte Vedra Concert Hall)
  • Relationship: 3 years (2023–2025)
  • Event: Local Showcase (month-long, downtown) + Live Wildly Showcase (2-day, Francis Field)
  • Crew: 4–5 (videographers, drone operator, photographers)
  • Delivered: per-day recaps within 48 hrs · 15–20 sec social clips · same-day photo edits · ~400 images (2025)

  • Festival scale (context, not ours): 34,000+ across the month · 25,000 at the Live Wildly Showcase · ~$9M local impact · $130K+ raised in charitable donations to Florida wildlife corridor preservation

First Sight Films has covered the Sing Out Loud Festival for three years running. Sing Out Loud is produced by SJC Cultural Events (SJCCE), the St. Johns County nonprofit that also runs the St. Augustine Amphitheatre and the Ponte Vedra Concert Hall — so the same team that trusts us with the festival trusts us with their venues. It's one piece of our broader St. Augustine video production work.

The festival comes in two parts, and we cover both — the free Local Showcase across downtown all September, and the paid Live Wildly main event at Francis Field, headlined by major national acts.

We help SJCCE plan the content capture so the turnaround keeps up with the event. We deliver a recap film for each day, cut social clips from every day — drone shots of the stage during the headliner, the big moments — and edit event photos same-day, pulling images right after an act steps off stage so the festival can post that night.

The work doesn't stop when the festival does. SJCCE stores our footage and reuses it for the next year's paid ad campaigns and organic social. Our festival and concert photography ran with local news coverage in the Ponte Vedra Recorder and the Jacksonville Business Journal, and drew strong engagement on the festival's own Instagram. We're the team SJCCE trusts to document it.

Cultural & Heritage Event Coverage: Fort Mose Jazz & Blues

February 2025 · Fort Mose Historic State Park

First Sight Films covered the Fort Mose Jazz & Blues festival for the St. Johns Cultural Events team — a night concert at Fort Mose Historic State Park, the site of the first free Black settlement in what's now the United States. The setting carries weight, and the coverage had to hold both the music and the history.

We ran a multi-camera setup on the performances and worked the grounds for the crowd, the dusk light, and the details that tie the night to the place. Because the event runs after dark, low-light shooting was the technical challenge — exposing for stage lighting while keeping the venue readable.

We delivered a recap film plus social cuts the team could run across their channels to carry the event's reach past the one night and into the next year's promotion. Read more about what it takes to capture live music at Fort Mose Jazz & Blues.

Ribbon Cuttings & Grand Openings

March 2026 · St. Augustine & St. Johns County Visitor Information Center

Project at a glance

  • Client: Consulate General of Ireland, Miami (found us via "event videographer in St. Augustine"; we replied same day)
  • Event: Irish Diaspora exhibit opening · St. Augustine Visitor Center · March 2026
  • Crew: 2 cameras
  • Captured: keynote speakers (flown in from Ireland), musical performances, attendees, b-roll
  • Delivered: 30-sec social reel + full captioned 2-angle keynote video
  • Result: ran as a paid month-long ad campaign promoting the exhibit

When the Consulate General of Ireland in Miami needed video for the opening of their St. Augustine exhibit, they Googled "event videographer in St. Augustine" and reached out to three of us. Two didn't answer for three weeks. First Sight Films answered the same day — and got the job.

First Sight Films filmed the opening of the exhibit on the Irish diaspora in St. Augustine at the St. Augustine & St. Johns County Visitor Information Center. The exhibit tells the story of the oldest known St. Patrick's Day parade in the Americas — which started right here in St. Augustine in 1600. The Consulate flew its speakers in from Ireland for the opening, including Irish Government Minister Niamh Smyth and faculty from University College Dublin and the Royal Irish Academy of Music.

We covered the full remarks from the keynote speakers, the musical performances, and attendees taking in the new exhibit, plus b-roll throughout. We delivered two pieces: a social reel built to advertise the exhibit and drive attendance, and a full keynote video of the two main speakers.
