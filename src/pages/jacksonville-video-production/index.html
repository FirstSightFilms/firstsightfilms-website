Corporate and Conference Video: HMA Mortgage Sales Rally

April 2025 · Embassy Suites, St. Augustine Beach

Project at a glance

  • Client: HMA Mortgage, a national lender licensed in 35+ states
  • Event: 2-day sales rally, Embassy Suites St. Augustine Beach, April 2025
  • Crew: 3 cameras, 1 stationary and 2 roaming
  • Captured: keynotes, breakouts, panels, Q&As, 12 attendee testimonials
  • Delivered: 30-second social reel the next day, then a 2-minute recap, 12 testimonials, and 4 product videos within the week, plus a custom animated logo

When HMA Mortgage flew its national team into Northeast Florida for a two-day sales rally, First Sight Films covered all of it. The venue was Embassy Suites St. Augustine Beach, an easy drive from Jacksonville International for their team and 45 minutes from downtown Jacksonville for ours. This is exactly the shape of the corporate work we do across the metro.

The challenge with a multi-day corporate event is that flown-in attendees sitting through sessions do not read as engaged on camera by day two. So we studied the agenda before the shoot, mapped the most interactive sessions, shot our best attendee b-roll on day one before fatigue set in, and mined the socials, breaks, and after-hours for energy. HMA had a social reel in hand the very next morning, while the rally was still the talk of their Slack.

Brand Story Films: Friends of Acadia and Rocky Mountain Conservancy

Acadia National Park, Maine · Rocky Mountain National Park, Colorado

Project at a glance

  • Clients: Friends of Acadia (Maine) and Rocky Mountain Conservancy (Colorado), the philanthropic partners of two national parks
  • Format: 60 to 90 second brand story films plus photography, 1-day and 2-day shoots
  • Approach: pre-shoot stakeholder meetings, real interviews, filming volunteers and locations on the ground
  • Outcome: FOA's film runs on its website and social channels; RMC screened its film to the Friends Alliance, the national coalition of park support organizations

Organizations have flown First Sight Films to Maine and Colorado to make their brand films. Jacksonville is a 40-minute drive, which is the point of this section.

For Friends of Acadia, the conservation nonprofit behind Acadia National Park, we shadowed volunteers on two real projects and interviewed the volunteer program director. She froze on camera. So Diego moved the interview into her car and filmed while she drove to the work site. With her mind off the lens, she relaxed and gave the film its story. "We love it, thank you so much," Julia Walker Thomas, the organization's visual assets manager, told us on delivery.

For Rocky Mountain Conservancy, we spent two days hiking gear into Rocky Mountain National Park to film the park's highlights and its newly built accessible boardwalks. RMC screened the finished film to the Friends Alliance, the national coalition of park support organizations, as it introduced itself as host of the group's next annual meeting.

The same process makes brand films for Jacksonville companies and nonprofits: a real interview, real work on screen, and a director who can get a story out of someone who hates being on camera.
