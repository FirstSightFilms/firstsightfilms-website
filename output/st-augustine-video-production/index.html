Corporate & Conference Video: HMA Mortgage Sales Rally

April 2025 · Embassy Suites, St. Augustine Beach

Project at a glance

  • Client: HMA Mortgage — national lender licensed in 35+ states
  • Event: 2-day sales rally
  • Crew: 3 cameras (1 stationary, 2 roaming)
  • Captured: keynotes, breakouts, panels, Q&As, 12 attendee testimonials
  • Delivered: 30-sec social reel next day; 2-min recap + 12 testimonials + 4 product videos within the week; custom animated logo

First Sight Films filmed HMA Mortgage's two-day Sales Rally at Embassy Suites St. Augustine Beach. HMA's a national mortgage lender licensed in 35+ states, and they flew their whole team in, so we covered all of it — full corporate event coverage across keynotes, breakouts, panels, Q&As, and the twelve attendee testimonials in between.

The hard part of a corporate rally isn't the filming — it's making a room full of people who've flown in, woken up early, and sat for eight hours actually look engaged. Tired doesn't read well on camera. So we planned around it: we studied the agenda ahead of time, mapped which sessions would be most interactive, and captured our best attendee b-roll on day one before the fatigue set in — then filled in the energy from the socials, the breaks, and the after-hours, where people actually connect.

Turnaround is where it counted — a social reel in their hands the very next day, then the recap, the testimonials, and four product videos inside the week, plus a custom animated logo they kept using.

"Wow, the videos that Diego and the team created for our corporate Sales Rally were absolutely spectacular! They completely captured our vibe (and definitely made us look cool!)! They were tuned into our goals and objectives, and delivered a product that went above and beyond!"

— Rachael Tresch, Director of Sales & Marketing Strategy, HMA Mortgage (Google, May 2025)

Brand Story Film: Friends of Acadia

2025 · Acadia National Park, Maine

Project at a glance

  • Client: Friends of Acadia — Maine conservation nonprofit
  • Goal: a 60–90 sec film + photography for web and social
  • Subject: the volunteer program that helps sustain the park — its history, how locals get involved, its impact
  • Access: interviewed the volunteer program director + shadowed volunteers on the carriage roads and a fence repair
  • Shoot: 1 day

A brand story film goes past what an organization does to why it exists. First Sight Films traveled to Maine to tell that story for Friends of Acadia — the nonprofit behind much of the volunteer work that keeps Acadia National Park running. We met with their team to plan the shoot, then spent a day on the ground: an interview with Nicki Burtis, their volunteer program director, and hands-on footage of volunteers clearing leaf litter from the historic carriage roads and mending a broken fence.

The interview didn't start smoothly — Nicki wasn't comfortable on camera. So we changed the setup. Instead of sitting her down under lights, we had her drive us out to the fence site and talked while she drove. Hands busy, eyes on the road, the camera disappeared — and she opened up. That's where the real story came from. It's the kind of read-the-room directing that separates footage from a film.

"We love it, thank you so much!"

— Julia Walker Thomas, Visual Storytelling & Visual Assets Manager, Friends of Acadia

Brand Story Film: Rocky Mountain Conservancy

2025 · Rocky Mountain National Park, Colorado

We produced the same kind of documentary brand film for the Rocky Mountain Conservancy, the philanthropic partner of Rocky Mountain National Park. Over two days, we hiked in with our gear to the spots they wanted to feature — the park's highlights and the newly added accessibility boardwalks that open the landscape to more visitors. The Conservancy went on to screen the film for the Friends Alliance, the national coalition of organizations that support America's national parks, using it to showcase their park and introduce their turn hosting the Alliance's next annual meeting. We travel nationally for mission-driven work like this.

Video Production FAQs

What types of video production does First Sight Films offer?

We produce corporate videos, brand story films, event coverage, festival recaps, and social media content. Our portfolio above includes work for businesses, nonprofits, music festivals, and cultural institutions across St. Augustine, Jacksonville, and beyond.

Where is First Sight Films based, and where do you film?

We're based in St. Augustine, FL and primarily serve Northeast Florida — including Jacksonville, Ponte Vedra, St. Johns County, and Duval County. There are no travel fees within 60 miles. We also travel nationally for select projects, including recent shoots in Maine (Friends of Acadia) and Colorado (Rocky Mountain Conservancy).

How much does a video production project cost?

Most projects range from $3,000 to $15,000 depending on scope, length, and crew size. We also offer monthly content retainers starting at $5,000 for organizations that need ongoing content. Reach out with your goals and we'll give you a clear, itemized quote.

How long does a typical video project take from start to finish?

Simple projects in St. Augustine or Jacksonville can be delivered in 3–5 days. Larger productions — like brand films, festival recaps, or multi-day corporate shoots — typically take 3–6 weeks from kickoff to final delivery. We start every project with a focused discovery call so the shoot day runs efficiently and revisions stay minimal.

Do you work with nonprofits and festivals?

Yes. A significant portion of our portfolio is nonprofit and festival work — including Friends of Acadia, Rocky Mountain Conservancy, Live Wildly + Florida State Park Foundation, Fort Mose Jazz & Blues Festival, Sing Out Loud Music Festival, and the St. Johns County Chamber of Commerce. We offer adjusted rates for qualifying mission-driven organizations.

What's included when I hire First Sight Films for a video project?

Everything end-to-end: creative direction, pre-production planning, filming, editing, color grading, music licensing, and revisions. You get a full production team — not just a camera operator. We also help with content strategy upfront, so you're investing in video that actually moves your business.

Can you produce video coverage for corporate events in St. Augustine?

Yes. Corporate event videography — sales rallies, conferences, galas, anniversaries — is one of our specialties. Recent work includes the HMA Sales Rally at Embassy Suites St. Augustine Beach and the SJC Chamber of Commerce 120th anniversary celebration. We can deliver a full recap, social cutdowns, and individual interview clips from the same shoot.

Can we use the video in our ads and campaigns?

Yes. You get a perpetual license to use everything we deliver for commercial purposes — website, social, and paid advertising. We keep ownership of the footage (and may feature the work in our own portfolio), but your right to run it in your marketing doesn't expire.

How do you make sure interviews and speaker audio are clean?

We capture multiple audio inputs for redundancy, so one failure never costs you the take. For a podium or stage, we pull a direct feed from the venue's audio board and add lav mics on speakers when they're there for the full remarks. Clean audio is half of what makes a video feel professional — we don't leave it to the on-camera mic.

Can you provide a certificate of insurance for our venue?

Yes. We carry $1 million in general liability insurance and can send a certificate of insurance ahead of your shoot, including naming your venue as additional insured if they require it.

What if something goes wrong with the gear?

We bring backups for everything — cameras, audio, power, and media. On multi-camera shoots, a single point of failure never costs you the moment. That redundancy is a big part of why clients trust us with once-only events they can't reshoot.

How far in advance should I book?

The sooner the better — we take a limited number of projects at a time, and dates fill first in busy stretches. If your shoot is soon, check the date with us now; we can sometimes fit in fast-turnaround work.

What makes you different from a freelancer or another production company?

Most companies hand you a video; we set out to deliver a result. We start from your goal, ask why until we understand it, and stay tenacious until the finished piece does the job — like redirecting a camera-shy interviewee until the real story comes out. You also get a full in-house team (creative direction, filming, editing, color, and music under one roof) and the trust of the institutions we already work with, from national lenders to the organizations that run St. Augustine's biggest venues.

How do I get a quote for my video project?

The fastest way is the contact form on our contact page — tell us what you're trying to accomplish, your timeline, and any examples you like. We'll respond within one business day with next steps and a ballpark quote.
