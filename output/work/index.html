No retainers required — the portfolio above includes single-day projects and four-year relationships alike.

How our clients work with us

Three ways projects happen — and one of them is staying on call.

Most of the work on this page started as a single project: one event, one film, one photo session. Some of it became ongoing — SJC Cultural Events, the nonprofit behind the St. Augustine Amphitheatre and Ponte Vedra Concert Hall, has kept us on call for four years across concerts, promos, and photography. And some of it is seasonal: Flagler College's graduation photography returns every May, and the Fort Mose Jazz & Blues Festival brings us back every February.

  • Single projects — one event or film, quoted to scope, delivered ready to publish.
  • Recurring coverage — annual festivals, graduation seasons, and event series that rebook year over year.
  • On call — a bespoke retainer for organizations that need video, editing, and photography on demand. We currently hold one such arrangement, and it has lasted four years.

Who we work with

Regional venues and institutions, and national brands.

  • Venues & festivals — the St. Augustine Amphitheatre and Ponte Vedra Concert Hall (both run by SJC Cultural Events, our on-call client of four years), and the Fort Mose Jazz & Blues Festival.
  • Colleges — Flagler College, where we've photographed graduation for three years.
  • Corporate — HMA Mortgage, a national lender whose two-day sales rally we covered with three cameras.
  • Nonprofits & parks — Friends of Acadia (Maine) and the Rocky Mountain Conservancy (Colorado), plus Live Wildly and the Florida State Park Foundation closer to home.
  • Civic & cultural — the St. Johns Cultural Council, the St. Johns County Chamber of Commerce's 120th anniversary, and the Consulate General of Ireland in Miami, whose Irish Diaspora exhibit opened here in St. Augustine.

Beyond Northeast Florida

The "national reach" in the numbers above is two real trips, not a tagline. Friends of Acadia — the philanthropic partner of Acadia National Park in Maine — brought us up to film the story of their volunteer program: one shoot day shadowing crews on the carriage roads, delivered as a brand film for their website and social channels. The Rocky Mountain Conservancy followed with a two-day shoot in Rocky Mountain National Park, Colorado, showcasing the park's newly accessible boardwalk trails; the Conservancy screened the finished film to the Friends Alliance, the national coalition of park support organizations. When the story is right, we travel to it.
