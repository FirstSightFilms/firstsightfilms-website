St. Augustine Event & Conference Venues: A Coverage Pro's Guide

St. Augustine packs an unusual range of event spaces into one small, historic city — oceanfront ballrooms, a 1888 former hotel, a 1920s bank with a vault bar, a state park, and one of Florida's best outdoor amphitheaters. First Sight Films covers events across all of them, so this guide is the planner's-eye view and the coverage view: what each venue is best for, how big it is, where it sits, and — for the ones we've filmed and photographed — what they're actually like to work. Use it to shortlist by event type and size, then talk to the venue for dates and pricing.

1. St. Augustine Amphitheatre

The region's premier outdoor venue, set inside Anastasia State Park, with a capacity of about 4,700 and 40-plus concerts a year alongside community and cultural events. If your event is a concert, festival, or large outdoor gathering, nothing else in St. Augustine matches its scale or setting. We're the Amphitheatre's house photographer, so we know its rhythms cold — the canopy and late-day light, the load-in flow, and where to be when the headliner walks out (here's a behind-the-scenes look). It's a coverage dream and a coverage challenge: big crowds, fast-changing stage light, and a sprawling footprint that rewards a team that's worked it before.

Address: 1340C A1A South, St. Augustine, FL 32080.

2. World Golf Village Renaissance St. Augustine Resort

The area's heavy hitter for conferences. The resort carries roughly 45,000 square feet of event space, including a 26,400-square-foot indoor ballroom that seats up to 3,000 — by far the largest in the St. Augustine area, with the breakouts, staging, and hotel rooms a multi-day conference needs. If you're running a true convention or a large general session, this is the short list. For coverage, the scale means multi-room, multi-camera planning — keynotes, breakouts, and an expo floor running at once.

Address: 500 S Legacy Trail, St. Augustine, FL 32092.

3. Casa Monica Resort & Spa

St. Augustine's landmark luxury hotel, a Moorish Revival icon downtown with about 12,000 square feet of event space and a Flagler Ballroom that hosts galas and seated dinners up to roughly 300. It's the choice when the brief is "historic and high-end" — award nights, executive dinners, upscale receptions. The interiors are rich and dramatic, which on camera means exposing carefully for ambiance rather than washing it out with flat light — a venue that rewards a team that knows how to shoot a moody historic room.

Address: 95 Cordova Street, St. Augustine, FL 32084.

4. Embassy Suites by Hilton St. Augustine Beach Oceanfront Resort

The go-to oceanfront conference hotel, with more than 20,000 square feet of meeting space and a 5,200-square-foot Anastasia Ballroom that holds up to 580 and splits into three breakout rooms — all with the Atlantic out the window. We filmed HMA Mortgage's two-day Sales Rally here, running keynotes and panels in the Anastasia Ballroom and out on the terrace, then pulling attendees into the Surf Crest Ballroom — the ocean and the St. Augustine Pier right behind them — for testimonials. It's a flexible, camera-friendly property with the rare bonus of a usable oceanfront backdrop.

Address: 300 A1A Beach Blvd, St. Augustine, FL 32080.

5. Flagler College (Lewis Auditorium)

A working college on a National Historic Landmark campus — the 1888 former Hotel Ponce de Leon, in Spanish Renaissance style. Lewis Auditorium seats about 795 and suits lectures, ceremonies, performances, and large presentations, while the broader campus offers some of the most striking historic backdrops in the city. We shoot here regularly — graduation coverage and architectural work in the rotunda and former hotel interiors — so we know which spaces photograph best and how to light the historic ones.

Address (Lewis Auditorium): 14 Granada Street, St. Augustine, FL 32084.

6. The Treasury on the Plaza

A 1920s former bank turned event space on the downtown plaza, complete with a grand ballroom and a vault bar, hosting up to about 450 guests (around 225 in the main ballroom). It's a distinctive pick for galas, receptions, and milestone celebrations that want character without leaving the historic district. The architecture does a lot of the work on camera — the trick is balancing the dramatic interior with clean, usable light on faces.

Address: 24 Cathedral Place, St. Augustine, FL 32084.

7. Lightner Museum

The former Alcazar Hotel, a Gilded Age treasure on King Street, available for receptions, award dinners, and private functions from intimate gatherings of 40 up to around 225. It's a setting you can't replicate — courtyards, columns, and museum galleries — ideal when the event itself is meant to feel like an occasion. Like the city's other historic interiors, it rewards coverage that exposes for the room's mood instead of flattening it.

Address: 75 King Street, St. Augustine, FL 32084.

8. Ponte Vedra Concert Hall (nearby, in Ponte Vedra Beach)

A short drive north, this restored historic hall is one of Northeast Florida's best mid-size indoor venues — a flexible room that runs from a 450-seat theater to a roughly 900-capacity standing concert hall. It suits concerts, seated performances, and upscale seated events. Run, like the Amphitheatre, by SJC Cultural Events, it's a venue we cover often; its low, moody stage lighting is the technical challenge, and we shoot it with exposure bracketing to keep the dark interior clean.

Address: 1050 A1A North, Ponte Vedra Beach, FL 32082.

9. Fort Mose Historic State Park

A site that carries real weight: the first legally sanctioned free African settlement in what's now the United States, now a state park that hosts cultural and heritage events — most notably the Fort Mose Jazz & Blues festival, which we cover. Coverage here is as much about honoring the place as the program: an after-dark outdoor concert means low-light shooting that has to hold both the music and the history. Note that events at a state park carry their own permit requirements.

Address: 15 Fort Mose Trail, St. Augustine, FL 32084.

10. Francis Field

St. Augustine's downtown festival grounds — a large open-air field beside the Castillo that hosts the city's biggest outdoor events, including the Sing Out Loud Festival's Live Wildly Showcase, which we cover with a multi-person crew and drone. It's the answer for a large public festival or community event in the heart of downtown. Coverage is festival-scale: roaming crews, fast same-day edits, and aerial when the permit allows. One planning note — there's no on-site parking, so factor in shuttles and the adjacent city garage.

Address: 29 W Castillo Drive, St. Augustine, FL 32084.

How to choose the right venue for your event

  • Large conference / convention: World Golf Village Renaissance (up to 3,000).
  • Oceanfront corporate event: Embassy Suites St. Augustine Beach.
  • Historic gala or awards dinner: Casa Monica, The Treasury on the Plaza, or Lightner Museum.
  • Concert or seated performance: Ponte Vedra Concert Hall.
  • Large outdoor festival or concert: St. Augustine Amphitheatre or Francis Field.
  • Cultural / heritage event: Fort Mose Historic State Park.
  • Lecture, ceremony, or campus event: Flagler College.

Once you've shortlisted a venue, the rest of the planning is covered in our other guides: what event coverage costs, the questions to ask before you book, and photo vs video for events.

FAQ

Which St. Augustine venue is best for a large conference?

The World Golf Village Renaissance, with ~45,000 sq ft of event space and a ballroom for up to 3,000 — the only area venue at true convention scale.

Where should I host a historic gala or awards dinner?

Casa Monica, The Treasury on the Plaza, or the Lightner Museum — all historic downtown settings built for upscale seated events.

Is there an oceanfront option for a corporate event?

Yes — Embassy Suites St. Augustine Beach, with 20,000+ sq ft of meeting space and a 5,200 sq ft oceanfront ballroom.

What's the best venue for a large outdoor festival?

The St. Augustine Amphitheatre (≈4,700) for a ticketed concert, or Francis Field for a downtown public festival.

Do you photograph and film events at these venues?

Yes — we cover events across all of them, and we're the house photographer for the St. Augustine Amphitheatre and a regular at the Ponte Vedra Concert Hall, Flagler College, and Fort Mose. See our event video coverage and event photography.
