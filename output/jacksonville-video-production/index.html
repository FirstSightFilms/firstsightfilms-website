Corporate and Conference Video: HMA Mortgage Sales Rally

April 2025 · Embassy Suites, St. Augustine Beach

Project at a glance

  • Client: HMA Mortgage, a national lender licensed in 35+ states
  • Event: 2-day sales rally, Embassy Suites St. Augustine Beach, April 2025
  • Crew: 3 cameras, 1 stationary and 2 roaming
  • Captured: keynotes, breakouts, panels, Q&As, 12 attendee testimonials
  • Delivered: 30-second social reel the next day, then a 2-minute recap, 12 testimonials, and 4 product videos within the week, plus a custom animated logo

When HMA Mortgage flew its national team into Northeast Florida for a two-day sales rally, First Sight Films covered all of it. The venue was Embassy Suites St. Augustine Beach, an easy drive from Jacksonville International for their team and 45 minutes from downtown Jacksonville for ours. This is exactly the shape of the corporate work we do across the metro.

The challenge with a multi-day corporate event is that flown-in attendees sitting through sessions do not read as engaged on camera by day two. So we studied the agenda before the shoot, mapped the most interactive sessions, shot our best attendee b-roll on day one before fatigue set in, and mined the socials, breaks, and after-hours for energy. HMA had a social reel in hand the very next morning, while the rally was still the talk of their Slack.

Brand Story Films: Friends of Acadia and Rocky Mountain Conservancy

Acadia National Park, Maine · Rocky Mountain National Park, Colorado

Project at a glance

  • Clients: Friends of Acadia (Maine) and Rocky Mountain Conservancy (Colorado), the philanthropic partners of two national parks
  • Format: 60 to 90 second brand story films plus photography, 1-day and 2-day shoots
  • Approach: pre-shoot stakeholder meetings, real interviews, filming volunteers and locations on the ground
  • Outcome: FOA's film runs on its website and social channels; RMC screened its film to the Friends Alliance, the national coalition of park support organizations

Organizations have flown First Sight Films to Maine and Colorado to make their brand films. Jacksonville is a 40-minute drive, which is the point of this section.

For Friends of Acadia, the conservation nonprofit behind Acadia National Park, we shadowed volunteers on two real projects and interviewed the volunteer program director. She froze on camera. So Diego moved the interview into her car and filmed while she drove to the work site. With her mind off the lens, she relaxed and gave the film its story. "We love it, thank you so much," Julia Walker Thomas, the organization's visual assets manager, told us on delivery.

For Rocky Mountain Conservancy, we spent two days hiking gear into Rocky Mountain National Park to film the park's highlights and its newly built accessible boardwalks. RMC screened the finished film to the Friends Alliance, the national coalition of park support organizations, as it introduced itself as host of the group's next annual meeting.

The same process makes brand films for Jacksonville companies and nonprofits: a real interview, real work on screen, and a director who can get a story out of someone who hates being on camera.

Jacksonville Video Production FAQs

How much does video production cost in Jacksonville?

Most of our projects run between $3,000 and $15,000, depending on scope. Event coverage has fixed tiers: event photography from $1,200, a half-day photo and video package at $2,000, a single-videographer video day from $2,500, full photo and video coverage from $5,500, and multi-day coverage from $9,500. Corporate videos and brand films are quoted to scope after a short call.

Do you charge travel fees for Jacksonville projects?

No. Jacksonville sits inside our standard 50-mile service radius, so travel is included for any project in Duval County. Our studio is in St. Augustine, about 40 minutes south on I-95, and we quote Jacksonville work at the same rates as work in our home county.

What kinds of video do you produce for Jacksonville businesses?

Corporate videos, conference and event coverage, brand story films, and short-form social content, plus commercial and event photography. Most Jacksonville inquiries are corporate: sales meetings, conferences, testimonial shoots, and recap films a marketing team can keep reusing.

Have you actually worked in Jacksonville?

Yes. We have photographed concerts at the Florida Theatre in downtown Jacksonville. Our festival photography has run in the Jacksonville Business Journal, and we produce ongoing promotional work for the Ponte Vedra Concert Hall on the metro's southern edge.

Can you cover a multi-day conference or corporate meeting?

Yes, that is the core of our corporate work. For HMA Mortgage's two-day sales rally we ran three cameras, covered the keynotes, breakouts, panels, and Q&As, filmed 12 attendee testimonials, and had a social reel in the client's hands the next morning, with the full recap package delivered inside a week.

Do you provide AV, staging, or livestreaming?

No. First Sight Films produces video and photo content, the assets you use after the event. We do not run event AV, staging, sound reinforcement, or livestreams. If your event needs an AV company, we work alongside the one you hire, and the division of labor is clean.

Who owns the footage, and can we run it in paid ads?

You get a perpetual license for commercial use of everything we deliver, from your website and social channels to paid advertising campaigns. First Sight Films retains ownership of the work and archives the raw files for three years, so re-edits are possible long after delivery.

How do you keep speaker audio clean?

We record multiple audio inputs so a single failure never costs you the speech. At conferences we pull a direct feed from the venue's audio board and add lav mics at the podium when speakers deliver full remarks. You get clean, usable speaker audio rather than room sound.

What happens if a camera or recorder fails mid-event?

Nothing you would ever notice. We carry backups for cameras, audio, power, and media, and multi-camera coverage means no single point of failure. Your event only happens once, so we build every shoot around that fact.

Can you provide a certificate of insurance for our Jacksonville venue?

Yes. We carry $1 million in general liability insurance and can send a certificate of insurance ahead of your event, including naming the venue as additional insured if it requires that.

How fast is turnaround?

Event clients get photos and a 20 to 30 second social recap within 72 hours on every tier. Next-day social reels are available, simple projects deliver in 3 to 5 days, and brand films or multi-day productions run 3 to 6 weeks. Our Event Story Rush tier delivers the full event package in 7 business days.

How far in advance should we book?

As early as you can. We take a limited number of dates and busy seasons fill first. If your event is close, ask anyway, because we are quick to answer. One client found us after two recommended vendors went silent for three weeks; we replied within 30 minutes and won the job.

Can you get drone footage of our Jacksonville event or property?

Sometimes. Drone work depends on the location and FAA rules. General-area aerial b-roll is sometimes available at no extra cost, and live drone coverage during an event is an add-on we quote per flight with licensed, insured operators.

Why hire a St. Augustine crew instead of a Jacksonville freelancer?

Because you are hiring a company: backups on every piece of gear, insurance your venue will accept, a second shooter, and a four-year track record with institutions that keep rebooking us. The real risk at an event is a crew with no backup plan, and that risk has nothing to do with a 40-minute drive.
